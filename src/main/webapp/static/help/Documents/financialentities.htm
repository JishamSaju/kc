Business Needs and Purpose

The Financial Entities screen is completed whenever:

•      University-employed researchers must report personal financial information to the institution for management of potential conflicts of interest

•      Reporters need to answer required institutional questions to comply with related policies regarding disclosure of potential conflicts of interest

The Financial Entities screen is used to:

•      Create, update, and delete reporters’ personal financial information about their relationships with entities outside the institution

•      Review disclosures of financial interests/relationship submitted by reporters in order to develop appropriate management plans to mitigate potential and/or actual conflicts of interest and thus keep the institution in compliance with applicable regulations.

Policy

In part to maintain objectivity and to eliminate bias in research, federal regulations and university policies require reporters to file conflict of interest disclosures (annually, event-based). Individual universities and institutions have various ways to determine and manage significant financial interests pertaining to individual reporters and specific projects. One way to manage conflict of interest is through the use of a questionnaire; a mechanism by which the reporter answers questions directly related to the proposed research.

The policy statements that pertain to this activity are:

•      Public Health Service:  The review and management of conflict of interest internal disclosure made by “Investigators” is required under 42 CFR 50 and 45 CFR 94 for projects funded by the Public Health Service.  In order to comply with these regulations, each institution must have in place a process to identify and manage conflicts of interests related to the ongoing research of the reporter.  In addition, the records related to conflicts of interest must be kept and accessible for at least three years after the date of the initial internal disclosure.

•      National Science Foundation:  The NSF requires each grantee institution employing more than fifty persons to maintain an appropriate written and enforced policy on conflict of interest. An institutional conflict of interest policy should require that each investigator disclose to a responsible representative of the institution all significant disclosable interests of the investigator (including those of the investigator’s spouse and dependent children) (i) that would reasonably appear to be affected by the research or educational activities funded or proposed for funding by NSF; or (ii) in entities whose disclosable interests would reasonably appear to be affected by such activities.

•      Additional COI Requirements:  Federal, state and local agencies & each research institution may have additional policies and/or regulatory requirements regarding reporting of Conflict of Interest.  It is incumbent upon participating institutions to assure that they are in full compliance with any and all current or future federal, state, or local agency requirements not detailed herein.

•      State COI Reporting:  Many states also impose conflict of interest reporting requirements such as the California Political Reform Act of 1974, which generally exceeds the conflict of interest reporting requirements imposed by federal agencies.   Each Kuali-Coeus institution is responsible for discovery of their applicable state rules and regulations related to conflict of interest laws and inclusion of same in their reporting processes.

Roles and Responsibilities

The primary responsibilities for each actor in this procedure are:

•      The Reporter is responsible for entering/selecting, submitting, and maintaining financial entity information

•      The COI Administrator is responsible for reviewing financial entity information submitted by the reporter, communications with the reporter regarding management and mitigation of potential conflicts of interest, and approving financial entities

The generic actor Reporter (view/edit) in this procedure may refer to any the following equivalent applicable job titles:

•      Researcher

•      Principal Investigator

Creating A New Financial Entity

Introduction

 

You complete the New Financial Entity section when you need to create a new financial entity because there is a potential conflict of interest.

Scope

This procedure covers:

•      Creating and submitting a new financial entity

•      Specifying relationship details

Before You Begin

Before you begin this procedure, you will first need the following:

•      Access:  permissions associated with the Reporter role

•      Time:  adequate time to complete (1/2 hr.?) due to the inability to save a work-in-progress

•      Pre-existing Conditions: the reporter must be a user of the system, but otherwise no records (existing data or documents in a particular state) need to be in place first in order to complete this

•      Prerequisite Tasks: 

The following tasks must be completed prior to initiating this activity:

•      A Reporter has answered one or more of the COI screening questions in such a way that they are required to provide more information about the specific financial entities with which they have a relationship.

Routing And Status

Financial Entities are not e-docs, and thus do not route or change status based on document completion or workflow approvals in the same way most KC e-docs do.

Workflow

Workflow status generally relates to approval-related actions - the routing of the completed record in the system for approval.  Even after approval and final disposition actions are completed, a permanent historical record of the Financial Entity is always accessible.

COI Committee Members review and approve disclosures with associated financial entities.  They may include the following roles:

•      Chair

•      Vice Chair

•      Expedited Reviewer

•      COI Coordinator/Administrator

•      Legal Counsel

•      Other Staff

•      IP/Tech transfer office staff

Financial Entities are records maintained separately, like KC maintenance e-docs, and made available for selection/association with Disclosure documents in KC.

Example Scenario

The following describes an example scenario of real-world functional business needs and how using the Financial Entities screen fullfills those needs.

When a reporter, or a member of the reporter’s immediate family, has a relationship with a financial entity, the details of that relationship must be disclosed.  When creating a new COI disclosure, you are first required to answer an initial series of screening questions.  If any of the answers you the reporter provide trigger the disclosure of financial entity and financial entity relationship details, then you will need to enter information about the names of the financial entity, or entities, that need to be disclosed.

You will be able to create, update or deactivate a financial entity and answer specific questions about the nature of your relationship with that entity.

You may not delete financial entities once they have been created.  You can only ‘deactivate’ an entity you no longer wish to include in your disclosure.  COI Administrators often need information about the financial entities their reporters disclose (for example, a company website, company type, or company address).
